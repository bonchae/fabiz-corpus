# Apex Supply Chain Announces Sale of International Freight Forwarding Business

*Press Release, November 2024*

Apex Supply Chain today announced a definitive agreement to sell its international freight forwarding business to Pelagic Holdings, a private equity firm, for $720 million in cash. The transaction is expected to close in Q1 2025, subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals.

The international freight forwarding business generated approximately $840 million in revenue over the trailing twelve months and contributed roughly $50 million in operating profit. The business has approximately 1,100 employees across 22 countries.

"This transaction allows Apex to sharpen our focus on our core North American domestic logistics business, where we see the greatest opportunity for growth and margin improvement," said CEO David Liu. "The proceeds will be used to reduce debt, invest in our domestic operations, and return capital to shareholders."

## Strategic rationale

Apex framed the sale as part of a broader repositioning toward its domestic trucking and warehousing segments. Management indicated that approximately $300 million of the proceeds will be used to reduce long-term debt, with the remainder split between domestic operational investment and share repurchases.

## Financial impact

The sale price of $720 million is below the $800 million threshold that at least one sell-side analyst had identified as the minimum acceptable outcome. On a conference call following the announcement, Liu defended the price as "reflecting current market conditions" and noted that the international freight forwarding business had faced "persistent headwinds" that made a sale the preferable option to continued investment.

Pro forma for the sale, Apex would have trailing-twelve-month revenue of approximately $2.0 billion, down from $2.84 billion on a reported basis.

## Analyst reaction

Initial analyst reaction was mixed. Some noted that the sale de-risks the company's balance sheet and simplifies the business. Others flagged concern that the reduced revenue base and the continued operational challenges in the Pacific Northwest leave Apex with limited strategic options.

Jameson Capital Research, which downgraded Apex to HOLD in October, maintained the rating following the announcement, citing continued "execution risk" on the core domestic business.

## Context on the Pacific Northwest

Notably, the announcement did not address the company's Pacific Northwest operational difficulties or the Seattle hub specifically. Analysts on the call asked about Seattle hub performance, and Liu indicated only that "operational improvements are underway" without providing specifics.

# NorthStar Freight Inc. — Q3 2024 Earnings Summary

*Internal analyst draft, October 2024*

Revenue for the quarter was $847 million, up 12% year-over-year. Operating margin compressed from 14.2% to 11.8%, driven primarily by higher fuel costs and a one-time $22 million charge related to the settlement of a labor dispute in the Pacific Northwest region.

Free cash flow was $61 million, down 8% from Q3 2023. The CFO attributed the decline to increased capital expenditure on electric vehicle fleet conversion (approximately $40 million in Q3) and higher working capital needs.

Management reaffirmed full-year guidance of $3.3–3.4 billion in revenue and noted that the EV transition is expected to pressure margins through 2025 before generating fuel-cost savings starting in 2026.

Key risks flagged on the earnings call:
- Further fuel price volatility
- Potential West Coast port congestion in Q4
- The unresolved status of a Teamsters contract negotiation affecting 3,200 drivers

CEO Marcus Reed emphasized that the Pacific Northwest remains a "strategic priority" and that the recent labor settlement "clears the path for operational stability through 2025."

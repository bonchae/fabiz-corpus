# Pacific Northwest Port Congestion Returns Ahead of Holiday Season

*Pacific Maritime News, October 2024*

Container volumes at the Ports of Seattle and Tacoma have surged 22% year-over-year in September, reigniting concerns about congestion as the region enters the peak holiday shipping season. Dwell times for containers at the Port of Tacoma averaged 5.8 days in September, up from 3.2 days in August and approaching the 6.5-day average seen during the 2021 supply chain crisis.

The Port of Seattle reported similar pressures, with terminal operators citing a combination of factors:

- Higher-than-expected volumes from Asian shippers repositioning inventory
- Rail capacity constraints on BNSF and Union Pacific lines
- A tight labor market for longshore workers and truck drivers
- Weather-related delays in early September

"This is not yet a crisis, but the trajectory is concerning," said port spokesperson Maria Chen. "We are working with carriers and shippers to manage the peak."

## Logistics company exposure

Logistics companies with significant Pacific Northwest operations are watching the situation closely. NorthStar Freight, which operates major terminals in both Seattle and Tacoma, has begun running 24-hour gate operations at its Tacoma facility. Meridian Logistics has reportedly diverted some inbound volume through its Portland distribution center to relieve pressure on its Seattle operations.

Apex Supply Chain's Seattle hub has been particularly affected, with industry sources reporting that Apex has missed delivery commitments on approximately 8% of its Pacific Northwest shipments in September. Apex declined to comment on specific metrics but acknowledged in a statement that "the current operating environment is challenging."

## Outlook

Port officials expect conditions to remain tight through December. Shippers and logistics companies are advised to plan for extended lead times and to consider alternative routings where possible.

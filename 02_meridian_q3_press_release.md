# Meridian Logistics — Q3 2024 Press Release

*For immediate release, October 2024*

Meridian Logistics today reported Q3 revenue of $1.2 billion, up 18% year-over-year, driven by continued expansion in the Pacific Northwest and strong performance in its cold-chain division. Operating margin expanded to 16.5%, up from 14.1% in Q3 2023.

CEO Janet Huang announced the acquisition of Pacific Cold Storage, a regional cold-chain operator with 14 facilities across Washington and Oregon, for $340 million in cash. The deal is expected to close in Q1 2025 subject to regulatory approval.

"This acquisition cements our leadership in the Pacific Northwest cold-chain market," Huang said. "We see meaningful synergies with our existing network and expect the deal to be accretive to earnings in 2026."

Meridian raised full-year revenue guidance to $4.7–4.8 billion, up from a prior range of $4.5–4.6 billion. The company also announced a $200 million share repurchase authorization.

Key operational highlights:
- Cold-chain segment revenue grew 31% year-over-year
- On-time delivery rate improved to 94.2% from 91.8%
- Fleet utilization reached 87%, a company record
- The company signed a 5-year contract with a major grocery retailer for Pacific Northwest distribution

Meridian operates 62 distribution centers across the western United States and employs approximately 14,000 people.

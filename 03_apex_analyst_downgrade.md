# Apex Supply Chain — Analyst Downgrade Note

*Jameson Capital Research, October 2024*

**Rating: HOLD (downgraded from BUY)**
**Price target: $42 (down from $58)**

We are downgrading Apex Supply Chain (APEX) from BUY to HOLD following disappointing Q3 results. Revenue of $680 million came in 6% below consensus, and operating margin contracted 180 basis points to 9.2%.

Management cited "unexpected softness in West Coast volumes" and pulled full-year guidance. We note that Apex's Pacific Northwest exposure is roughly 35% of revenue, and the region appears to be the primary drag. Competitors NorthStar and Meridian both reported stronger Q3 results in the same geography, suggesting share loss rather than market weakness.

Our channel checks indicate Apex lost at least two mid-sized customers in the Pacific Northwest during Q3 to Meridian, citing reliability concerns at Apex's Seattle hub. Seattle hub on-time performance has reportedly fallen below 85% for three consecutive months.

Risks to our HOLD rating:
- Further market share erosion in the Pacific Northwest
- Unresolved labor issues at the Seattle hub (informal work stoppages reported in August and September)
- Execution risk on the planned divestiture of the international freight forwarding business, expected to close in Q1 2025
- Potential covenant pressure if Q4 comes in weaker than guided

We would become more constructive on the stock if: (1) Seattle hub reliability metrics recover to above 90%, (2) the international divestiture closes at or above $800 million, or (3) the company provides updated guidance with visible cost actions.

# NorthStar Freight — Internal Memo on EV Fleet Transition

*From: Sarah Okonkwo, VP of Fleet Operations*
*Date: September 2024*
*Status: Confidential — Executive Leadership*

## Summary

We remain on track to have 18% of the long-haul fleet converted to electric vehicles by end of 2025, consistent with our investor-day commitment. However, two developments warrant leadership attention.

## Cost pressures

Our Q3 capital expenditure for EV conversion came in at $40 million, approximately 14% above plan. The overrun is attributable to:

1. Charging infrastructure costs at the Portland and Tacoma terminals ran $9 million over budget due to utility interconnection fees we had not anticipated
2. Battery pack prices did not decline as projected; our supplier revised 2024 pricing upward in July
3. Driver training costs increased as we expanded the program to include all three West Coast regions

Full-year EV capex is now tracking to $155–165 million, versus the $140 million we guided at the beginning of the year.

## Operational progress

Despite cost pressures, operational metrics are ahead of plan. The 87 EVs currently in service have demonstrated a 22% lower cost-per-mile than comparable diesel units in the Pacific Northwest corridor, driven primarily by fuel cost differentials and reduced maintenance. The Tacoma-to-Portland route is now 100% electric for dedicated service.

## Risk flagged

We are increasingly concerned about the Teamsters contract negotiation. The EV transition requires significant changes to driver workflows, charging schedules, and route planning. If the contract is not resolved by Q1 2025, we risk both the EV rollout timeline and broader operational stability.

## Recommendation

Request $15 million in supplemental capex authority for Q4 2024 to maintain the rollout schedule and begin charging infrastructure work at the Seattle terminal in November rather than Q1 2025.

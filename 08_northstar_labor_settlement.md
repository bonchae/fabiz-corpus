# NorthStar Freight Settles Pacific Northwest Labor Dispute

*Trade Publication: Fleet Owner Weekly, September 2024*

NorthStar Freight has reached a settlement with Teamsters Local 174 covering 1,850 drivers and terminal workers in the Pacific Northwest, ending a dispute that had triggered two short work stoppages in August.

The settlement, which is separate from the broader Teamsters contract negotiation affecting 3,200 NorthStar drivers nationally, includes:

- A one-time $22 million payment to resolve back-pay claims and grievances
- Wage increases of 4.5% in 2024 and 3.5% in each of 2025 and 2026
- New scheduling protocols for long-haul routes
- Commitment to retrain affected drivers for EV fleet operations rather than reduce headcount as the EV transition proceeds

CEO Marcus Reed called the settlement "a fair outcome that reflects the importance of our drivers to our Pacific Northwest operations." Teamsters Local 174 President Rebecca Moss said the agreement "addresses the immediate concerns of our members while preserving jobs through the fleet transition."

## Context

The settlement resolves a dispute that had created operational disruptions in August, when approximately 400 NorthStar drivers participated in a two-day work stoppage at the Tacoma terminal. A second, shorter stoppage occurred at Portland in late August.

The broader national Teamsters contract negotiation remains unresolved. That contract, which covers approximately 3,200 NorthStar drivers across the company, is scheduled to expire in February 2025. Industry observers view the Pacific Northwest settlement as a possible template for the larger negotiation.

## Market reaction

Analyst reaction to the settlement was mixed. The one-time $22 million charge will pressure Q3 earnings, and the wage increases will add an estimated $18 million to annual labor costs. However, analysts also noted that resolving the immediate dispute removes a significant operational risk during the peak shipping season.

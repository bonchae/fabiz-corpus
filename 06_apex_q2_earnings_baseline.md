# Apex Supply Chain — Q2 2024 Earnings Summary

*Published July 2024*

Apex Supply Chain reported Q2 revenue of $712 million, up 2% year-over-year, in line with consensus expectations. Operating margin was 11.0%, flat versus Q2 2023 and down 40 basis points sequentially from Q1.

The company maintained full-year revenue guidance of $2.85–2.95 billion and operating margin guidance of 10.5–11.5%.

## Segment performance

- **Domestic trucking:** $420 million (+4% YoY), margin pressure from driver wages and fuel
- **International freight forwarding:** $210 million (-3% YoY), continued softness in trans-Pacific volumes
- **Warehousing and distribution:** $82 million (+6% YoY)

## Key commentary

CEO David Liu acknowledged "elevated competitive intensity" in the Pacific Northwest region but characterized it as "a temporary dynamic" and reaffirmed the company's commitment to the region. Management noted that Q3 trends through mid-July were "consistent with seasonal patterns."

Liu also announced the company is exploring strategic alternatives for the international freight forwarding business, including a potential sale or spin-off, with a decision expected by end of 2024.

## Analyst Q&A highlights

An analyst asked whether Meridian's cold-chain expansion posed a threat to Apex's Pacific Northwest business. Liu responded that Apex's customer base is "sticky" and that the company "has not seen meaningful share loss," though he acknowledged "increased pricing pressure from well-capitalized regional competitors."

Another analyst asked about the Seattle hub, where industry publications had reported operational difficulties. Liu stated that the Seattle hub was "performing in line with our expectations" and declined to provide specific metrics.

## Context

This report should be read alongside the subsequent Q3 results, which came in materially below this guidance and led to a downgrade from at least one sell-side firm.

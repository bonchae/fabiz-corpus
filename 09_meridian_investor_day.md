# Meridian Logistics — Investor Day Summary

*September 2024, Seattle WA*

Meridian Logistics hosted its annual investor day on September 18, 2024, with presentations from CEO Janet Huang, CFO Daniel Park, and heads of the three operating segments.

## Long-term targets

Management reaffirmed the company's 2027 financial targets:
- Revenue of $6.0–6.5 billion (implying a 7–9% CAGR from 2024)
- Operating margin of 17–18%
- Free cash flow conversion above 70%
- Return on invested capital of at least 15%

## Strategic priorities

Huang articulated four strategic priorities:

1. **Pacific Northwest density.** Meridian will continue investing in regional density rather than national expansion. "We want to be the undisputed leader in one region, not a mediocre national player."

2. **Cold chain as a growth engine.** Cold chain segment revenue is expected to reach $1.0 billion by 2026, roughly 50% growth from current levels. The Pacific Cold Storage acquisition is central to this target.

3. **Technology investment.** Meridian announced a $200 million multi-year investment in routing, warehouse management, and customer-facing technology. Management expects this to generate 150–200 basis points of operating margin improvement by 2027.

4. **Disciplined capital allocation.** The company committed to returning at least 50% of free cash flow to shareholders through dividends and buybacks.

## Pacific Northwest commentary

Multiple analysts asked about competitive dynamics in the Pacific Northwest. Huang characterized the competitive environment as "favorable" for Meridian, citing:

- NorthStar's distraction with EV transition costs and labor negotiations
- Apex's operational difficulties at its Seattle hub
- Meridian's investment in Portland as a secondary hub to Seattle

"We are taking share," Huang said. "We expect to continue taking share through 2025."

## Risks mentioned

Park identified several risks in the CFO presentation:

- Regulatory review of the Pacific Cold Storage acquisition
- Integration risk associated with rapid cold-chain expansion
- Wage pressure across the logistics industry
- The possibility of a broader economic slowdown affecting freight volumes

## Stock reaction

Meridian shares rose 4.2% on the day of the presentation, outperforming the transportation sector index.
